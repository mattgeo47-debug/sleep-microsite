About PSQI Scoring
The Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index (PSQI) is a clinically validated self-report questionnaire that measures sleep quality over a period of time. It evaluates seven components: subjective sleep quality, sleep latency, sleep duration, habitual sleep efficiency, sleep disturbances, use of sleep medication, and daytime dysfunction. A PSQI global score ranges from 0 to 21 — lower scores indicate better sleep quality (a score of 5 or below is considered good sleep). Our system converts your PSQI score into a Longevity Sleep Index (0–100) so it can be compared alongside device-based scores on the leaderboard.
